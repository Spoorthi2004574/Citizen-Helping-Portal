How to get UDID & Disability Certificate

What is UDID & Disability Certificate?

The UDID (Unique Disability ID) is a government-issued unique identity for persons with disabilities. The Disability Certificate documents the type and percentage of disability and is used to access benefits, schemes, concessions and reservations.

Eligibility

  • Person with a diagnosed disability covered under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act / state norms.
  • No age limit — children and adults can apply.
  • Assessment required to determine type & percentage of disability.

Documents Required

  • Photo ID (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport)
  • Proof of address (any govt-issued address proof)
  • Recent passport-size photograph
  • Medical records or prior disability certificates (if any)
  • Birth certificate / school record for children (if applicable)

Exact document list may vary by state/hospital — check the local UDID portal or the chosen assessment hospital.

Step 1 — Submit Application

  1. Create an account and fill the UDID application on the official portal (state UDID / central UDID portal).
  2. Enter accurate personal details and upload the required documents and photo.
  3. Double-check details and submit. Note the application/acknowledgement number.

Example portal: UDID Portal (example)

Step 2 — Visit Selected Hospital / CMO Office

  1. Once status shows Submitted, visit the chosen hospital's UDID/Disability Certification section.
  2. Get your application verified and follow instructions for any corrections or additional documents.

Step 3 — Verification & Assessment

  1. The hospital/medical authority will verify your documents.
  2. Specialist doctor(s) will be assigned for clinical assessment on the scheduled date.
  3. Attend the assessment — doctors prepare a detailed evaluation report.

Step 4 — Medical Board Review

  1. The Medical Board reviews the specialist report(s).
  2. They determine the disability type and percentage, and validate the certificate.

Step 5 — UDID Card Generation & Dispatch

  1. After approval, the UDID card and Disability Certificate are generated by the authority.
  2. The Department (DEPwD / concerned state authority) or hospital will dispatch the UDID card to your registered address (often via Speed Post).

Processing & dispatch timelines vary by state / hospital — keep tracking with your acknowledgement number.

Track Application & Follow-up

  • Use the acknowledgement / application number on the UDID portal to track status.
  • If delayed, contact the hospital UDID section or the state nodal officer.
  • Keep scanned copies of all uploaded documents and the acknowledgement for reference.

Important Notes

  • Assessment outcome (type & percentage) is final for that certificate period — renewal rules differ by state.
  • Carry originals during hospital visit for verification.
  • If issued by mistake or if details are incorrect, request corrections as per portal/hospital process.

Helpful Contacts & Links

  • UDID Portal (central/state) — check your state’s e-portal
  • Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD)
  • Local Medical Board / Nodal Officer at the chosen hospital
