How to Apply for a Domicile Certificate in India

What is a Domicile Certificate?

A Domicile Certificate proves you are a permanent resident of a particular state or union territory in India. It is required for education, jobs, property registration, and government schemes restricted to state residents.

Documents Often Required Alongside

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Voter ID
  • PAN Card
  • Driving License

Eligibility

You must have lived in the state for the period specified by local laws (often 10–15 years) or meet special criteria defined by the state government.

How to Apply Online

  1. Visit your state’s official e-Governance portal.
    • Delhi: edistrict.delhigovt.nic.in
    • Maharashtra: aaplesarkar.mahaonline.gov.in
  2. Create an account with your details (name, mobile, email, Aadhaar if required).
  3. Login and search for “Domicile Certificate” or “Permanent Residence Certificate”.
  4. Fill the application form with accurate personal and address details.

Documents to Upload

  • Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID)
  • Address proof (utility bill, rent agreement)
  • Age proof (birth certificate, 10th certificate)
  • Passport-size photograph

Fees & Processing Time

  • Application fee: ₹50–₹100 (via UPI, card, or net banking)
  • Processing time: 7–10 working days
  • Certificate issued online or via email
