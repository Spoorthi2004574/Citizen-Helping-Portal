Understanding Caste Certificates & How to Apply

What is a Caste Certificate?

There are many castes in India. A caste certificate helps legally establish which caste you belong to. It is an important document, especially for those in ‘Scheduled Caste’ or ‘Scheduled Tribe’ categories, as it enables access to government schemes, reservations, and benefits.

How to Apply for a Caste Certificate

You can apply either online or offline. Below are step-by-step processes for both methods.

Online Application Process

  1. Visit your state or Union Territory’s (UT) Backward Class Welfare Portal.
  2. Click on “Apply for Caste Certificate Online.”
  3. Create a new account or log in if you already have one.
  4. Fill in the application form with accurate personal and contact details.
  5. Verify details, accept terms and submit the form.
  6. Upload scanned documents as required.
  7. Submit and note the acknowledgement slip and application number.
  8. Track your application status using the application number.
  9. After approval, download the caste certificate from the portal.

Offline Application Process

  1. Visit your nearest Tehsil Office, Revenue Office, SDM Office, or CSC / SETU centres.
  2. Obtain the caste certificate application form.
  3. Fill in personal, address, and contact details.
  4. In the ‘Caste Detail’ section, provide your father’s caste certificate details or those of blood relatives if father is deceased.
  5. If migrated from another state, mention it and submit a self-declaration.
  6. Attach a passport size photograph and local references for verification.
  7. Submit the completed form with required self-attested documents.
  8. Carry original documents for verification during the process.
  9. Certificate will usually be delivered within 30-35 days at your registered address.

Documents Required for Caste Certificate

Most states require these documents to prove your address, identity, age, and caste.

  • Proof of Identity (POI)
  • Proof of Address (POA)
  • Birth Certificate
  • Proof of Caste
  • 2 Passport-size Photos
  • Affidavit for Caste Certificate
  • Properly filled-in Application Form

Documents for Proof of Identity (POI)

  • Aadhaar Card (UID)
  • Passport
  • PAN Card
  • Voter ID
  • Driving License
  • Other recognized government-issued ID

Documents for Proof of Address (POA)

  • Passport
  • Voter ID
  • Ration Card
  • Driving License
  • House Registration Document
  • Aadhaar Card (UID)
  • Lease Agreement
  • Telephone/Electricity/Water Bill
  • Self Declaration attested by Gazette Officer
  • Flat Allotment/Possession Letter
  • Other recognized government-issued documents

Documents for Proof of Caste

  • Applicant’s and father/grandfather’s high school admission record
  • Applicant’s or father’s primary school leaving certificate
  • Applicant’s/father’s/relatives’ birth certificate
  • Government Service Record showing caste/community group
  • Caste certificate issued by Department of Social Justice
  • Validity certificate issued by scrutiny committee (if applicable)
  • Village Panchayat or tax records
  • Documentary proof of caste & ordinary residence prior to caste notification
  • Other documents issued by competent authority

Additional Documents Depending on Scenario

  • Case 1: No evidence of father’s caste — provide relative’s caste certificate & genealogical affidavit.
  • Case 2: Married woman — proof of caste before marriage, marriage certificate, and gazette name change notice.
  • Case 3: Migrated from another state/district — submit father’s caste certificate from that state/district authority.
  • Case 4: Religious conversion — provide proof of caste prior to conversion.

How to Apply for Caste Validity Certificate

  1. Request the caste validity certificate application form from the Tehsil or SDM office.
  2. Complete and submit the form properly.
  3. Wait for up to a month to receive the certificate.

Note: The validity period of caste certificates varies by state; some certificates are lifelong, others may need periodic renewal.
