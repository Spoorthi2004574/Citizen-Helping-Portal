How to Apply for Vehicle Registration Certificate in India

Introduction

A Vehicle Registration Certificate (RC) is an official document issued by the Regional Transport Office (RTO) that serves as proof of ownership and registration of a vehicle. It contains details such as registration number, make, model, engine number, chassis number, and owner information.

Documents Required for RC

  • Proof of Identity
  • Proof of Age
  • Vehicle Purchase Invoice
  • Copy of Vehicle Insurance
  • Copy of PAN Card
  • Temporary Registration from Dealer
  • Receipt for Registration Fees
  • Form 20, Form 21, Form 22/22-A, Form 34

How to Apply for RC

  1. Visit your nearest RTO with your vehicle
  2. Vehicle inspection by authority
  3. Fill and submit Form 20
  4. Chassis number imprint by RTO
  5. Submit required documents
  6. Pay registration fees
  7. RC dispatched by post

You can also apply through a dealer with a valid trade certificate and online access to the RTO system.

Getting a Duplicate RC

  1. File an FIR at the nearest police station
  2. Fill Form 26 for duplicate RC
  3. Get financier’s signature if under loan
  4. Submit documents to RTO

Documents for Duplicate RC

  • FIR Copy
  • Proof of Road Tax Payment
  • Insurance Certificate
  • No Objection Certificate (if financed)
  • Pollution Under Control Certificate

How to Track RC Status Online

  1. Visit the Parivahan Portal
  2. Click 'Online Services' → 'Vehicle Related Services'
  3. Select your state and RTO
  4. Click 'Status' → 'Know Your Application Status'
  5. Enter application number and submit
  6. View RC details online
