How to Apply for Driving License in India

Introduction

A Driving License is a legal document required to operate motor vehicles in India, governed by the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. It is issued by the Regional Transport Office (RTO).

Types of Driving Licenses

  • Learner’s License: Temporary, issued before permanent license
  • Permanent License: After passing driving test
  • Commercial License: For taxis, trucks, buses
  • International Driving Permit: For driving abroad

Eligibility Criteria

  • 16+ years: Two-wheelers without gear (with consent)
  • 18+ years: Two-wheelers with gear & four-wheelers
  • 20+ years: Commercial vehicles
  • Learner’s license is mandatory before permanent license

Documents Required

  • Application Form (Form 1 & 2 for Learner, Form 4 for Permanent)
  • Age Proof (Aadhaar, Birth Certificate, School Certificate)
  • Address Proof (Voter ID, Passport, Utility Bill)
  • Learner’s License (for permanent license)
  • Medical Certificate (Form 1A) for 40+ or commercial license

Apply for Learner’s License

  1. Visit Parivahan Portal or RTO
  2. Fill the form and upload documents
  3. Book a slot for online test
  4. Pass the test to get learner’s license (valid for 6 months)

Apply for Permanent License

  1. Apply after 30 days of getting learner’s license
  2. Book a driving test appointment at RTO
  3. Appear for the driving test
  4. Get the permanent license after passing the test

Renewal of License

  1. Apply before expiry (valid for 20 years or until age 50)
  2. Fill Form 9 and submit required documents
  3. Medical certificate for 40+ years
  4. Pay renewal fee online or at RTO

Penalties

  • Driving without a license: Fine up to ₹5,000
  • Expired license: Additional penalties
  • Fake license: Legal consequences, possible imprisonment
