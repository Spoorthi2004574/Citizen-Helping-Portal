How to Apply for PAN Card

What is PAN?

PAN (Permanent Account Number) is a 10-character alphanumeric code issued by the Income Tax Department for financial transactions and identity verification.

Who can apply?

Any Indian citizen or foreign individual/entity engaged in financial transactions in India can apply for PAN. PAN is mandatory for income tax filing, bank accounts, and high-value transactions.

Steps to Apply Online

  1. Visit NSDL Website or UTIITSL
  2. Select New PAN (Form 49A) for Indian Citizens
  3. Fill in the application details and upload required documents
  4. Pay the application fee online
  5. Submit the form and receive an acknowledgment number

Steps to Apply Offline

  1. Download or collect Form 49A
  2. Fill in the form and attach necessary documents
  3. Submit at the nearest PAN Service Center
  4. Pay the application fee
  5. Receive an acknowledgment slip for tracking

Required Documents

Identity Proof:

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Passport
  • Voter ID

Address Proof:

  • Bank Statement
  • Utility Bill

DOB Proof:

  • Birth Certificate
  • School Certificate

Application Fees

  • Physical PAN (India): ₹101
  • Physical PAN (Abroad): ₹1,011
  • e-PAN via Email: ₹66

Corrections / Updates

  1. Visit NSDL or UTIITSL portal
  2. Select "Correction in PAN" option
  3. Update details and upload documents
  4. Pay the correction fee and submit

Check PAN Status

  1. Go to NSDL/UTIITSL website
  2. Enter acknowledgment number
  3. Track your PAN application status online
