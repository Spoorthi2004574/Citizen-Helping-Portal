How to Apply for Birth Certificate

What is a Birth Certificate?

A Birth Certificate is an official document issued by the government that records a person's date, place of birth, and parentage. It serves as proof of identity, age, and nationality.

Who can apply?

Parents, legal guardians, or the individual (if of legal age) can apply for a birth certificate. For newborns, the application must be made within 21 days of birth.

Steps to Apply Offline

  1. Visit the local Registrar of Births and Deaths office
  2. Collect and fill the birth registration form
  3. Attach required documents (proof of birth, parents' ID & address proof)
  4. Submit the form for verification
  5. Collect the certificate after approval (usually 7–15 working days)

Steps to Apply Online

  1. Visit the official state portal or CRS Portal
  2. Register/Login to your account
  3. Fill the online application form
  4. Upload scanned copies of required documents
  5. Pay the applicable fee online
  6. Track status via SMS or email

Required Documents

  • Proof of birth (hospital discharge summary or doctor's certificate)
  • Parents' identity proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport)
  • Parents' address proof (electricity bill, ration card)
  • Marriage certificate of parents (if applicable)

Application Fees

  • Within 21 days: Nominal fee (varies by state)
  • Late registration: Additional penalty fee

Late Registration Process

  1. Submit application with affidavit explaining delay
  2. Approval required from a magistrate (for very late cases)
  3. Pay applicable late fees

Digital Birth Certificate

Many states now provide a downloadable digital birth certificate from their portals, which is legally valid and can be used for various purposes.
